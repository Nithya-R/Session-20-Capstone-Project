# Level 10 Civics – Panchayati Raj & Rural Development (Advanced)

*(Deep dive into how villages are governed and developed)*

## 1. The Vision of Local Self-Government

Decentralization means taking power away from the Central and State governments and giving it to local bodies. This ensures that people have a direct say in decisions that affect their daily lives. In rural India, this is achieved through the **Panchayati Raj System**, strengthened by the 73rd Amendment Act of 1992.

## 2. The Three-Tier Structure

The Panchayati Raj operates at three levels:

### Gram Panchayat (Village Level)
- The primary unit of governance.
- Consists of a Ward Member (Panch) and a President (Sarpanch) elected by the Gram Sabha.

### Panchayat Samiti (Block Level)
- A group of several Gram Panchayats forms a Block or Mandal.
- Coordinates development activities across many villages.

### Zilla Parishad (District Level)
- The highest tier at the district level.
- Makes developmental plans for the entire district and distributes government funds among the Panchayats.

## 3. The Role of the Gram Sabha

The Gram Sabha is the assembly of all adults living in a village. It is the foundation of the Panchayati Raj.
- Approves the annual budget of the Panchayat.
- Reviews the performance of the elected members.
- Identifies beneficiaries for government welfare schemes.
This is **Direct Democracy** in action.

## 4. Key Functions and Resources

Panchayats are responsible for:
- Constructing and maintaining village roads, water sources, and schools.
- Levying and collecting local taxes.
- Executing government schemes related to employment (e.g., MGNREGA).

**Sources of Funds**:
- Government grants from the State and Centre.
- Taxes on houses, marketplaces, etc.
- Donations for community work.

## 5. Reservation for Women and Marginalized Groups

To ensure fair representation, seats in the Panchayati Raj are reserved for:
- **Scheduled Castes (SC) & Scheduled Tribes (ST)** in proportion to their population.
- **Women**: At least one-third (33%) of all seats and chairperson positions are reserved for women. This has empowered millions of women in rural leadership roles.

## 6. Key Ideas from Level 10 Civics

After completing this level, learners should understand:
- The historical significance of the 73rd Amendment.
- The difference between Gram Sabha and Gram Panchayat.
- How the three tiers of rural governance coordinate with each other.
- The financial sources that power village development.
- How reservations have changed the social landscape of rural leadership.
