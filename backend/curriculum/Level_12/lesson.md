# Level 12 Civics – Social Justice & Marginalization

*(Ensuring equality and dignity for all sections of society)*

## 1. What is Marginalization?

To be marginalized means to be forced to the fringes of society. Marginalized groups often have less access to resources, education, and power. This can be due to their language, religion, caste, or economic status. In India, historically marginalized groups include Adivasis, Dalits, certain religious minorities, and women.

---

## 2. Marginalized Groups in India

### Adivasis (Tribal Communities)
Literally meaning "original inhabitants," Adivasis have deep connections with forests. Industrialization and mining have often led to their displacement and loss of traditional livelihoods.

### Dalits
Historically treated as "untouchables," Dalits faced severe social exclusion and were denied access to temples, water sources, and education for centuries.

---

## 3. Constitutional Protections for Equality

The Constitution of India uses laws to ensure social justice:
* **Article 15**: Prohibits discrimination on grounds of religion, race, caste, sex, or place of birth.
* **Article 17**: Formally abolished "Untouchability" and made its practice a punishable crime.
* **The 1989 Act**: The Scheduled Castes and the Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act was passed to punish those who humiliate or ill-treat these communities.

---

## 4. The Policy of Reservation

Reservation is a form of **Affirmative Action**. It means saving a percentage of seats in educational institutions, government jobs, and legislatures for SCs, STs, and Other Backward Classes (OBCs).

**Why do we need it?**
* To provide opportunities to those who were historically denied them.
* To ensure that all sections of society have a voice in decision-making.

---

## 5. Social Justice in Action

Social justice is achieved not just through laws, but through active government policies:
* **Scholarships** for students from marginalized backgrounds.
* **Hostel facilities** to encourage higher education.
* **Special funding** for small businesses started by SC/ST entrepreneurs.

---

## 6. Key Ideas from Level 12 Civics

After completing this level, learners should understand:
* the social and economic meaning of marginalization
* the specific challenges faced by Adivasis and Dalits
* how Articles 15 and 17 protect the dignity of citizens
* the purpose and logic behind the reservation system
* the role of specific laws like the 1989 Atrocities Act in seeking justice
