# Level 13 Civics – The Governor and State Executive

*(Understanding how individual states in India are governed)*

## 1. Structure of the State Government

Just like the Union Government at the Centre, each State in India has its own executive branch. The State Executive is responsible for managing the affairs of the state and implementing laws passed by the State Legislature.

The State Executive consists of:
- The Governor
- The Chief Minister
- The State Council of Ministers
- The Advocate General of the State

---

## 2. The Governor: The Constitutional Head

The Governor is the nominal head of the state, similar to the President's role at the Centre.

- **Appointment**: Appointed by the President of India for a term of 5 years.
- **Dual Role**: The Governor acts as the constitutional head of the state and also as a vital link between the Union Government and the State Government.
- **Powers**: All executive actions of the state are taken in the Governor's name. The Governor appoints the Chief Minister and other ministers.

---

## 3. The Chief Minister (CM): The Real Head

The Chief Minister is the real executive head of the state government.

- **Selection**: The Governor appoints the leader of the party or coalition that has a majority in the State Legislative Assembly (Vidhan Sabha).
- **Role**: The CM heads the State Council of Ministers, allocates portfolios, and is the chief advisor to the Governor.
- **Authority**: The CM is the primary face of the state government and leads the formulation of state policies.

---

## 4. The State Council of Ministers

The Council of Ministers, led by the CM, assists in governing the state. Like the Centre, the state council follows the principle of **Collective Responsibility**. They are answerable to the State Legislative Assembly. If the assembly passes a no-confidence motion, the entire council, including the CM, must resign.

---

## 5. Relationship between Centre and State Executives

While states have autonomy over subjects in the "State List," the Governor's office ensures that the state functions according to the Constitution. If there is a breakdown of constitutional machinery, the Governor can recommend "President's Rule" (Article 356), where the Centre takes over the state's administration.

---

## 6. Key Ideas from Level 13 Civics

After completing this level, learners should understand:
- the components of the State Executive
- the role of the Governor as a constitutional head and a link to the Centre
- how the Chief Minister is chosen and their primary powers
- the concept of collective responsibility at the state level
- how the Centre can intervene in state administration during a crisis
