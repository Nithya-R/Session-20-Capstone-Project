# Level 6 Civics – The Union Executive

*(The arm of government responsible for implementing laws and governing the country)*

## 1. What is the Executive?

The Executive is the branch of government responsible for the implementation of laws and the day-to-day administration of the country. In India, we have a **Parliamentary Executive**, where the head of government (Prime Minister) is part of the legislature and accountable to it.

The Union Executive consists of:
* The President
* The Vice-President
* The Prime Minister
* The Council of Ministers
* The Attorney General of India

---

## 2. The President of India

The President is the **Head of State** and the first citizen of India.

* **Position**: Nominal or Constitutional Head. Most powers are exercised on the advice of the Prime Minister.
* **Election**: Elected indirectly by an Electoral College (MPs and MLAs).
* **Term**: 5 years.
* **Key Powers**: Appoints the PM, Judges, Governors; Supreme Commander of Armed Forces; power to grant pardons.

---

## 3. The Prime Minister (PM)

The Prime Minister is the **Head of Government** and the most powerful functionary in the Indian system.

* **Appointment**: The President appoints the leader of the party having a majority in the Lok Sabha as the PM.
* **Role**: Heads the Council of Ministers, presides over Cabinet meetings, and acts as the chief link between the President and the Cabinet.
* **Power**: Decides the portfolios of ministers and coordinates the work of all departments.

---

## 4. The Council of Ministers

The Council of Ministers is a large body consisting of:
* **Cabinet Ministers**: Senior leaders in charge of major ministries (Home, Defense, Finance, etc.).
* **Ministers of State**: Assist Cabinet ministers or hold independent charge of smaller departments.

**Collective Responsibility**: The council "swims or sinks together." If a no-confidence motion is passed against one minister or the government in the Lok Sabha, the entire Council of Ministers must resign.

---

## 5. Civil Services: The Permanent Executive

While politicians (President, PM) are the "Political Executive" and change with elections, the **Permanent Executive** consists of civil servants (IAS, IPS, etc.) who provide continuity. They are responsible for detailed implementation of government policies and remain in office regardless of which party is in power.

---

## 6. Key Ideas from Level 6 Civics

After completing this level, learners should understand:
* the distinction between the Head of State (President) and Head of Government (PM)
* how the President and PM are chosen in the Indian system
* the concept of Collective Responsibility
* the hierarchy within the Council of Ministers
* the role of civil servants in the continuous functioning of the government
