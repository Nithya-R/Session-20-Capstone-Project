# Level 7 Civics – The Indian Judicial System

*(The branch of government that interprets laws and delivers justice)*

## 1. Role of the Judiciary

The Judiciary is an independent branch of government that interprets the Constitution and resolves disputes. Its main functions include:
* **Dispute Resolution**: Between citizens, between states, or between citizens and the government.
* **Judicial Review**: The power to cancel laws passed by Parliament if they violate the Constitution.
* **Enforcing Rights**: Protecting the Fundamental Rights of citizens.

---

## 2. Independence of the Judiciary

In India, the Judiciary is **independent**. This means:
* The Legislature and Executive cannot interfere in the work of the courts.
* Judges are appointed based on merit and cannot be easily removed by politicians.
* This independence ensures that the "Rule of Law" is maintained and no one is above the law.

---

## 3. Structure of Courts in India

India has a **unified judicial system**, meaning decisions made by higher courts are binding on lower courts.

### Supreme Court of India
The highest court, located in New Delhi. It is headed by the **Chief Justice of India (CJI)**. Its decisions are final and applicable to the entire country.

### High Courts
The highest judicial body at the State level. Currently, there are 25 High Courts in India.

### District and Subordinate Courts
Courts at the district or tehsil level where most people first go for justice. They deal with local civil and criminal cases.

---

## 4. Criminal vs. Civil Law

The legal system is divided into two main branches:

**Criminal Law**: Deals with conduct defined as offenses against the state or society (e.g., theft, murder, harassment). It usually begins with an FIR (First Information Report) at a police station.

**Civil Law**: Deals with disputes related to the rights of individuals (e.g., land disputes, rent matters, divorce, contract breaches). A petition is filed in the relevant court by the affected party.

---

## 5. Public Interest Litigation (PIL)

A unique feature of the Indian system is the PIL. It allows any individual or organization to file a case on behalf of others who cannot reach the court (e.g., the poor or marginalized). This has made justice more accessible to common people.

---

## 6. Key Ideas from Level 7 Civics

After completing this level, learners should understand:
* why an independent judiciary is vital for democracy
* the hierarchy of the Indian court system (Supreme, High, District)
* the power of Judicial Review as a check on other branches
* the difference between criminal and civil cases
* how PIL has expanded access to justice in India
