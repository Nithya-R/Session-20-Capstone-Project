# Level 15 Civics – Public Policy and Governance Watchdogs

*(The institutions that ensure transparency, merit, and accountability)*

## 1. The Need for "Watchdogs"

For a democracy to function well, we need independent institutions that keep an eye on the government, ensure that public money is spent correctly, and that the best people are chosen for public service. These are called "General Watchdogs" or Regulatory Bodies.

---

## 2. CAG: The Guardian of the Public Purse

The **Comptroller and Auditor General (CAG)** of India is a constitutional authority (Article 148).
* **Role**: The CAG audits all the money spent by the Central and State governments.
* **Importance**: It ensures that every rupee of the taxpayers' money is spent for the intended purpose and follows legal procedures.
* **Accountability**: The CAG submits reports to the Parliament, which are then scrutinized by the Public Accounts Committee.

---

## 3. UPSC: The Merit-Based Guardian

The **Union Public Service Commission (UPSC)** is the premier recruiting agency for the Government of India.
* **Role**: It conducts examinations (like the Civil Services Exam) to select officers for the IAS, IPS, and other All-India Services.
* **Importance**: It ensures that government jobs are given based on **merit** and talent, rather than political favoritism.
* **Independence**: The Chairman and members are appointed by the President and have high job security to prevent political interference.

---

## 4. RTI: Empowering the Citizen

The **Right to Information (RTI) Act, 2005** is a revolutionary law that gives every citizen the right to ask the government for information.
* **How it works**: Any citizen can file an "RTI Application" asking for details about government projects, spending, or decisions.
* **Timeline**: The government must provide the information within 30 days.
* **Impact**: It has greatly reduced corruption and made government officials more careful and transparent.

---

## 5. Other Key Watchdogs

* **Central Election Commission**: Ensures fair elections (covered in Level 9).
* **National Human Rights Commission (NHRC)**: Protects citizens against abuses of power.
* **Central Vigilance Commission (CVC)**: Specifically deals with preventing corruption within government departments.

---

## 6. Key Ideas from Level 15 Civics

After completing this level, learners should understand:
* why independent "watchdogs" are necessary in addition to the three branches of government
* the CAG's role in auditing and financial transparency
* how the UPSC maintains the quality and neutrality of the civil services
* the power of the RTI Act in the hands of an ordinary citizen
* how these institutions collectively protect the "health" of Indian democracy
