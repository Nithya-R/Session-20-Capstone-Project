# Level 9 Civics – Elections and Political Parties in India

*(The mechanics of the world's largest democracy)*

## 1. Why Do We Have Elections?

In a large democracy like India, it is impossible for all citizens to sit together and make every decision. Therefore, we elect representatives to make laws and run the government on our behalf. Elections ensure that the government remains accountable—if the people are unhappy, they can change the leaders in the next election.

---

## 2. What Makes an Election Democratic?

A democratic election must follow these principles:
* **Universal Adult Franchise**: Every citizen aged 18 and above has one vote, and every vote has equal value.
* **Real Choice**: There must be multiple parties and candidates for voters to choose from.
* **Regular Intervals**: Elections must happen every few years (usually 5 years in India).
* **Free and Fair**: People should be able to vote without fear or pressure.

---

## 3. The Election Commission of India (ECI)

The ECI is an independent body that conducts elections in India. It is extremely powerful and works without government interference.
* It prepares the **Voters' List**.
* It announces the election schedule.
* It implements the **Model Code of Conduct** (rules that parties must follow during elections).
* It monitors election spending and ensures fair play.

---

## 4. Understanding Political Parties

A political party is a group of people who come together to contest elections and hold power in the government.

**Main Functions of Parties**:
* **Contest Elections**: Nominate candidates to run for office.
* **Policy Formulation**: Put forward different programs and ideologies for the public to choose from.
* **Forming Government**: The party with a majority forms the government.
* **Role of Opposition**: Parties that lose keep a check on the government by criticizing wrong policies.

---

## 5. Types of Party Systems

* **National Parties**: Parties that have a presence in many states and are recognized by the ECI based on their performance (e.g., BJP, Congress).
* **Regional/State Parties**: Parties that operate primarily within one or a few states (e.g., DMK, TMC, AAP).
* **Coalition Government**: When no single party gets a majority, multiple parties join together to form a government.

---

## 6. Key Ideas from Level 9 Civics

After completing this level, learners should understand:
* why elections are the "festival of democracy"
* the role of the Election Commission in ensuring fairness
* how a voter list is prepared and updated
* the meaning and importance of political parties
